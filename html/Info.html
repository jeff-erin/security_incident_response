Malware:
Software that is designed to disrupt, damage, or gain unauthorized resources from your computer. Telltale signs of a malware attack can include: computer displaying strange behaviour, pop ups that you don't recognize and didn't open, unusual error messages.

Phishing Email:
Emails that are sent to your inbox trying to lure you into clicking illegitimate links or attatchments that can infect your computer with malware or obtain critical information. Telltale signs of a phishing email include: misspelled sender address, emails trying to induce panic or quick decision making, emails that provide links to change a password or enter private informtaion.

Quid pro Quo:
Attacker is requesting information in return for a gift or providing a service. Examples of quid pro quo attacks include the hacker posing as part of the IT staff or a research group and asking for network access or other critical info in exchange for a gift card or other beneficial items/services.
